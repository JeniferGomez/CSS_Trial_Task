Did you know? During the historic Apollo 11 mission in 1969, when Neil Armstrong became the first human to walk on the lunar surface, he famously uttered the phrase "That's one small step for [a] man, one giant leap for mankind." ." However, in the live broadcast, the word "a" in the sentence seemed to be masked by static, making the sentence sound like "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind." Despite this, Armstrong claimed that he had actually said "That's one small step for one man," and it has become one of the most iconic quotes in the history of space exploration.
